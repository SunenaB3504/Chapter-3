‚úñÔ∏è Multiplying Bigger Numbers (Like 42 x 36) ‚úñÔ∏è

Multiplying bigger numbers might seem tricky, but it's just a few simple steps repeated!

Let's break down how to multiply a 2-digit number by another 2-digit number.

We'll use the example: 42 x 36

Step 1: Multiply by the Ones Digit

First, multiply the top number (42) by the ones digit of the bottom number (which is 6).

Think: 42 x 6

   42
 x 36
 ----
  252  <-- (This is 42 x 6)

(How we got 252: 6 x 2 = 12. Write down 2, carry over 1. Then 6 x 4 = 24. Add the carried-over 1 to get 25. Write down 25.)

Step 2: Multiply by the Tens Digit

Next, multiply the top number (42) by the tens digit of the bottom number (which is 3, but it really means 30).

Think: 42 x 30

To do this, first put a zero in the ones place on the next line (because we are multiplying by tens).

Then, multiply 42 by 3.

   42
 x 36
 ----
  252
 1260 <-- (This is 42 x 30)

(How we got 1260: Put down the 0. Then 3 x 2 = 6. Write down 6. Then 3 x 4 = 12. Write down 12.)

Step 3: Add the Products

Finally, add the results from Step 1 and Step 2 together.

   42
 x 36
 ----
  252  <-- (42 x 6)
+1260  <-- (42 x 30)
 ----
 1512  <-- (The final answer!)

So, 42 x 36 = 1512.
