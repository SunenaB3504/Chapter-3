ğŸ¤” What is Multiplication? (It's Fast Adding!) ğŸ¤”

Imagine Aadya and Anvi buying beads. Each packet has 20 beads.

If they buy 6 packets, how many beads will they get?

We can add the beads from each packet:

Packet 1 + Packet 2 + Packet 3 + Packet 4 + Packet 5 + Packet 6

20 + 20 + 20 + 20 + 20 + 20 = 120 beads

That's a lot of adding! There's a faster way called Multiplication.

When we add the same number again and again, we can multiply.

We added the number 20, 6 times.

So, we can write it like this:

20 x 6 = 120 beads

We read this as "Twenty times six equals one hundred twenty".

The 'x' symbol means 'times' or 'multiply'.

Meet the Multiplication Team!

In 20 x 6 = 120:

  • The first number (20) is called the Multiplicand (the number being multiplied).
  • The second number (6) is called the Multiplier (how many times you multiply).
  • The answer (120) is called the Product.

So, Multiplication is just a shortcut for repeated adding!
