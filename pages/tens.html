Multiplying by numbers like 10, 100, or 1000 is super easy! It's like adding zeros.

Multiplying by 10

To multiply a number by 10, just put one zero (0) at the end of the number.

7 x 10 = 70

94 x 10 = 940

185 x 10 = 1850

30 x 10 = 300

Multiplying by 100

To multiply a number by 100, just put two zeros (00) at the end of the number.

5 x 100 = 500

4 x 100 = 400

23 x 100 = 2300

307 x 100 = 30700

60 x 100 = 6000

Multiplying by 1000

What about numbers like 20, 300, or 4000?

It's almost the same! Multiply the numbers without the zeros first, then add the total number of zeros from both numbers at the end.

11 x 20:

First do 11 x 2 = 22.

There is one zero in 20.

So, 11 x 20 = 220.

12 x 300:

First do 12 x 3 = 36.

There are two zeros in 300.

So, 12 x 300 = 3600.

25 x 3000:

First do 25 x 3 = 75.

There are three zeros in 3000.

So, 25 x 3000 = 75000.

50 x 4000:

First do 5 x 4 = 20.

There is one zero in 50 and three zeros in 4000 (total four zeros).

So, 50 x 4000 = 200000 (which is 200,000).
